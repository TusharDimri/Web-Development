Pum, who just graduated, returns home to find her mother leaving to go out with a younger man while her father is ill upstairs. As they were arguing, her father tried to come downstairs, but along the way, he fell and died. Pum is furious at her mother. Ramet, who's mother is handicapped, does everything for his mother, but she has a very cruel heart. Ramet’s mother wants a grandchild, so she puts out an ad looking for a girl who will have Ramet's baby and have nothing to do with them. They will grant the woman 5 million dollars in return. Ramet doesn’t like this at all, but he agrees in order to make his mother happy. The two meet on bad terms at the mall when Ramet's girlfriend wanted to buy the shirt that Pum got, but as she is friends with Ramet's brother Rangsan, Pum begins working at their family's modeling agency. After, Pum’s dad’s funeral, her mother's boyfriend moves in with them, against Pum’s wishes. Pralee and her mother have many arguments and disagreements until her mother kicks her out. After a fight at a party for Ramet's mother, Pum decides to apply for the ad in order to get back at her mother. She does not know that the man in the ad is Ramet. And so according to the deal, the two are sent to an island together, where they fall in love. Edit Translation

First off, except for the last paragraph, the synopsis is wrong. I understand the 2017 version is a remake, so maybe the description is for the original, but this version deals more with what happens in their lives and relationship after they get off the island than anything before the island.

I did finish the drama, but mostly because I was oddly curious about whether or not the male lead, Met, would ever accept the reality of how horrible his mother actually was. Turns out, he knew the extent of her manipulation, but allowed it to continue because of gratitude. Not dealing with his mother, led him to making the same mistakes over and over, which kept him from finding happiness with the female lead. The last third of the drama was watching him repeat the same cycle and it was hard to really sympathize because it was a disaster of his own making.

I didn't enjoy the drama, but I didn't hate it enough to give up in the middle. I did, however, fast forward through large chunks I didn't care about just to reach the end. As a side note, my favorite character was Paun, Pum's "gatekeeper" at the island house. She was realistic and relatable, even when she wasn't the nicest of characters.
